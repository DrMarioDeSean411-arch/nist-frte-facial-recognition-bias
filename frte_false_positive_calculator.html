NIST FRTE 1:1 Verification · Demographic Differentials

Operational false positive calculator

Estimates expected false matches when searching a facial recognition database, by demographic group and algorithm. Based on empirically measured false match rates from NIST's Face Recognition Technology Evaluation (2023–2025 submissions).

What this shows: Facial recognition algorithms produce false matches — cases where the system incorrectly claims two photos of different people show the same person. False match rates (FMR) vary substantially across demographic groups. This calculator applies the binomial approximation FPIR ≈ N × FMR (Grother, NISTIR 8429, Annex B, eq. 32) to convert per-comparison error rates into expected false positive counts for a database of N people.

Why it matters: In law enforcement or border control contexts, each false match is a potential wrongful investigation or detention. The disparity in expected false positives across demographic groups — not just the raw rate — reveals the operational consequence of demographic bias.

Limitation: This calculator uses the simplified binomial approximation. Actual 1:N false positive identification rates depend additionally on cross-demographic FMR, database composition, and search volume (see NISTIR 8429 Annex B, eqs. 33–37). For 1:N algorithms that stabilize the tail of the impostor distribution, FPIR may not scale linearly with N.
