How to read this chart: The bottom-left corner is ideal — low error, low inequity. The Pareto frontier (green dashed line) marks the boundary: no algorithm to the upper-left exists in the data. The amber band marks the observed floor for the best-performing submissions. The teal dashed line marks true parity (Gini = 0 or Max/GeoMean = 1.00). The gap between the amber band and the teal line is structural — it has not closed from 2022 to 2025 despite improved overall accuracy (Grother, 2022; NIST, 2026). Toggle the Y-axis to switch between the Gini coefficient (SAIC/DHS measure, eq. 13–14 in NISTIR 8429) and the Max/GeoMean ratio (NIST's preferred leading measure, eq. 10–11).

Methodology: FNMR overall, FNMR Max, Max/GeoMean, and Gini values are taken directly from the NIST FRTE demographic differential table (columns as documented in NISTIR 8429 §4 and the FRTE results page). Algorithms listed are a representative selection of publicly evaluated submissions covering the accuracy range. The Pareto frontier is computed as the lower-left convex hull of the plotted points — the set of algorithms not dominated on both FNMR and inequity simultaneously. The "best-algo floor" band spans the range of Gini (0.07–0.10) or Max/GeoMean (1.05–1.15) values observed among the top-performing algorithms (FNMR ≤ 0.0016) in the 2024–2025 submission cohort. Color encodes the worst-performing demographic group as reported in the FNMR Max column of the NIST table.
